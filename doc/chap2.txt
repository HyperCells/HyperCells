  
  [1X2 [33X[0;0YTriangle Groups and Translation Subgroups[133X[101X
  
  
  [1X2.1 [33X[0;0YTriangle Groups[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YTriangle  groups  are  stored  as  objects  of  category  [10XTriangleGroup[110X. The
  triangle  group is characterized by its signature [23X(r,q,p)[123X, with the integers
  [23Xr[123X, [23Xq[123X, [23Xp[123X by convention given in ascending order. It is defined as[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\Delta(r,q,p)                         =                         \left\langle
  a,b,c\left|a^2,b^2,c^2,(ab)^r,(bc)^q,(ca)^p\right.\right\rangle.[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0YTo construct a triangle group, use the function [2XTriangleGroup[102X ([14X2.1-1[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSimilarly, proper triangle groups are stored as [10XProperTriangleGroup[110X objects.
  They too are characterized by their signature [23X(r,q,p)[123X, and defined as[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\Delta^+(r,q,p)                        =                        \left\langle
  x,y,z\left|xyz,x^r,y^q,z^p\right.\right\rangle,[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0Ywhere  the  generators  [23Xx[123X, [23Xy[123X, [23Xz[123X are related to the generators [23Xa[123X, [23Xb[123X, [23Xc[123X of the
  corresponding  triangle  group  by  [23Xx  =  ab[123X, [23Xy = bc[123X, [23Xz = ca[123X. To construct a
  triangle group, use the function [2XProperTriangleGroup[102X ([14X2.1-2[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe two objects are printed in the form[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[28XTriangleGroup( [ r, q, p ] )[128X[104X
    [4X[28XProperTriangleGroup( [ r, q, p ] )[128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0Yrespectively.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YGiven a [10XTriangleGroup[110X or [10XProperTriangleGroup[110X object [3Xtg[103X, its signature can be
  obtained  by the operation [2XSignature[102X ([14X2.1-3[114X) and the (proper) triangle group
  itself can be obtained by the operation [2XFpGroup[102X ([14X2.1-4[114X).[133X
  
  [1X2.1-1 TriangleGroup[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XTriangleGroup[102X( [3Xsignature[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ya  [10XTriangleGroup[110X  object  representing  the  full  triangle  group
            [23X\Delta[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YConstruct the full triangle group with signature [3Xsignature[103X given as a list [10X[
  r, q, p ][110X:[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\Delta(r,q,p)                         =                         \left\langle
  a,b,c\left|a^2,b^2,c^2,(ab)^r,(bc)^q,(ca)^p\right.\right\rangle.[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe generators are named [10Xa[110X, [10Xb[110X, and [10Xc[110X, respectively.[133X
  
  [1X2.1-2 ProperTriangleGroup[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XProperTriangleGroup[102X( [3Xsignature[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ya  [10XProperTriangleGroup[110X  object  representing  the  proper triangle
            group [23X\Delta^+[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YConstruct the proper triangle group with signature [3Xsignature[103X given as a list
  [10X[ r, q, p ][110X:[133X
  
  
  [24X[33X[0;6Y\Delta^+(r,q,p)                        =                        \left\langle
  x,y,z\left|xyz,x^r,y^q,z^p\right.\right\rangle.[133X
  
  [124X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe generators are named [10Xx[110X, [10Xy[110X, and [10Xz[110X, respectively.[133X
  
  [1X2.1-3 Signature[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XSignature[102X( [3Xtg[103X ) [32X operation[133X

  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XFpGroup[102X( [3Xtg[103X ) [32X operation[133X

  [1X2.2 [33X[0;0YTranslation Groups[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YTriangle-group  translation subgroups are implemented as objects of category
  [10XTGTranslationGroup[110X  and store the underlying proper triangle group [23X\Delta^+[123X,
  the  quotient  homomorphism  [23X\Delta^+  \to  \Delta^+/\Gamma[123X, the translation
  group  [23X\Gamma[123X  as  a subgroup of [23X\Delta^+[123X and as a finitely presented group.
  Additionally,  the  group  isomorphism from [23X\Gamma[123X to the finitely presented
  group  is  stored.  A  [10XTGTranslationGroup[110X object can be constructed from the
  proper  triangle  group [23X\Delta^+[123X and a quotient group [23X\Delta^+/\Gamma[123X with a
  translation       subgroup       [23X\Gamma[123X       using       the       function
  [2XTGTranslationGroupFromQuotient[102X ([14X2.2-1[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YTriangle-group translation subgroups are printed in the form[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[28XTranslationGroup( < g1, g2, ... | rel1, rel2, ... > )[128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0Ywhere  [10Xg1[110X,  [10Xg2[110X,  ...  are the generators of the finitely presented group and
  [10Xrel1[110X, [10Xrel2[110X, ... are the relators.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  components  of  a  [10XTGTranslationGroup[110X  object can be accessed using the
  following operations:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XGetProperTriangleGroup[102X ([14X2.2-3[114X)[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XQuotientHomomorphism[102X ([14X2.2-4[114X)[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XAsTGSubgroup[102X ([14X2.2-5[114X)[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XFpGroup[102X ([14X2.2-6[114X)[133X
  
  [1X2.2-1 TGTranslationGroupFromQuotient[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XTGTranslationGroupFromQuotient[102X( [3XD[103X, [3XG[103X, [3Xgenus[103X[, [3XGAMgens[103X[, [3XTDGAM[103X[, [3XTGGw[103X]]] ) [32X function[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ythe translation group as a [10XTriangleTranslationGroup[110X object.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YConstruct  the  translation group as the kernel of the quotient homomorphism
  from  the  proper  triangle  group  [3XD[103X to the quotient group [3XG[103X. The functions
  makes  sure  that  a  minimal  number  of  translation generators is used by
  ensuring the number matches [3X2*genus[103X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YOptionally,  generators  [3XGAMgens[103X  of  the  translation  group  [23X\Gamma[123X can be
  explicitly  given  as  elements  of  [3Xtg[103X.  If  not  given,  they are computed
  automatically.  Similarly, choices for the transversals [23XT_{\Delta^+}(\Gamma)[123X
  and  [23XT_{G^+}(G_w^+)[123X  can  be  given  as [3XTDGAM[103X and [3XTGGw[103X, respectively. If not
  given, they are computed automatically. Here, [3XTDGAM[103X is a list of elements of
  [3Xtg[103X  and  [3XTGGw[103X  is  a  list of three lists, one for [23Xw=x,y,z[123X, respectively, of
  elements of [3Xtg[103X.[133X
  
  [1X2.2-2 TGTranslationGroup[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XTGTranslationGroup[102X( [3Xtg[103X, [3Xquotient[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ythe translation group as a [10XTriangleTranslationGroup[110X object.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YConstruct  the  translation group as the kernel of the quotient homomorphism
  from the proper triangle group [3Xtg[103X given as a [10XProperTriangleGroup[110X object (see
  [14X2.1[114X) to the quotient group [3Xquotient[103X given as a [10XTGQuotient[110X object (see [14X2.3[114X).[133X
  
  [1X2.2-3 GetProperTriangleGroup[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XGetProperTriangleGroup[102X( [3XGamma[103X ) [32X operation[133X

  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XAsTGSubgroup[102X( [3XGamma[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  
  [33X[0;0Yreturns  the  translation  group  [3XGamma[103X as a subgroup of the proper triangle

  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XFpGroup[102X( [3XGamma[103X ) [32X operation[133X

  [1X2.3 [33X[0;0YTriangle-Group Quotients[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YTriangle-group  quotients  [23X\Delta^+/\Gamma[123X  are  implemented  as  objects of
  category [10XTGQuotient[110X with the following components:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XTGQuotientName[102X ([14X2.3-2[114X): name of the triangle group quotient[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XTriangleGroupSignature[102X  ([14X2.3-4[114X):  signature of the underlying triangle
        group[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XTGQuotientGenus[102X ([14X2.3-3[114X): genus of the triangle group quotient[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XTGQuotientOrder[102X ([14X2.3-5[114X): order of the quotient group[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XTGQuotientActionType[102X ([14X2.3-6[114X): type of the action of the triangle group
        quotient[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[2XTGQuotientRelators[102X  ([14X2.3-7[114X):  defining  relators  of the quotient as a
        string[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIt is printed in the form[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[28X  TGQuotient( name, [ r, q, p ], order, genus, actiontype, relators )[128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0Ywhere  [10Xname[110X  is  the name of the triangle group quotient, [10X[ r, q, p ][110X is the
  signature  of  the  underlying  triangle  group,  [10Xorder[110X  is the order of the
  quotient  group,  [10Xgenus[110X  is  the  genus  of  the  triangle  group  quotient,
  [10Xactiontype[110X  is the type of the action, and [10Xrelators[110X is the defining relators
  of the quotient.[133X
  
  [1X2.3-1 TGQuotient[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XTGQuotient[102X( [3Xquotient[103X[, [3Xsignature[103X] ) [32X function[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ytriangle-group quotient as [10XTGQuotient[110X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YConstructs  the triangle group quotient of the triangle-group with signature
  [10Xsignature[110X,  given  as  a  list  [10X[ r, q, p ][110X, specified by [10Xquotient[110X, which is
  either an integer giving the position in Conder's list or a list of the form
  [10X[genus,  number][110X  giving  the  genus  and  number  in  Conder's  list of the
  quotient. In the latter case, signature can be omitted.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YLoads  all  quotient groups of the corresponding triangle group if they have
  not been loaded before.[133X
  
  [1X2.3-2 TGQuotientName[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XTGQuotientName[102X( [3Xtgquotient[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  
  [33X[0;0Yreturns  the  name  of  the  triangle group quotient. For quotients from the
  library, i.e., Conder's list, this takes the form [10X[genus, number][110X, [10Xnumber[110X is
  a running index over all triangle groups for the given genus.[133X

  [1X2.3-5 TGQuotientOrder[101X

  [1X2.3-6 TGQuotientActionType[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XTGQuotientActionType[102X( [3Xtgquotient[103X ) [32X operation[133X
  
  [33X[0;0Yreturns the type of the action of the triangle group quotient, following the
  terminology of Marston Conder described in [Con07].[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  action  is  called  [10X"reflexible  [m]"[110X  if  there  exists  an involutory
  automorphism  of  the  quotient  that inverts the images of two of the three
  generators [23Xx[123X, [23Xy[123X and [23Xz[123X. This is the same as hypermap reflexibility.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  action  is  called  [10X"reflexible  [n]"[110X  if two or more of [23Xp[123X, [23Xq[123X and [23Xr[123X are
  equal,  and  there  exists  an  involutory automorphism of the quotient that
  swaps  the images of two of the three generators [23Xx[123X, [23Xy[123X and [23Xz[123X with the inverse
  of  the  other.  For  example,  this  happens  if  [23Xp  = q[123X and the involutory
  automorphism  swaps the image of [23Xx[123X with the image of [23Xy^{-1}[123X, and vice versa.
  This  is  equivalent  to an extension of the [23X(p,p,r)[123X triangular action to an
  action of a larger group with NEC signature [23X(0; +; [p]; {(r)})[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn all other cases the action is irreflexible, or [10X"chiral"[110X.[133X
